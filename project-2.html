Yinzer Security

A prototype for an app that uses time series forecasting to predict crime in Pittsburgh

Introducing Yinzer security app that utilizes time series models, specifically the Exponential Smoothing (ETS) method, to predict crime in Pittsburgh. The app aims to provide people with more informed decisions about security in the area.

The ETS method is a statistical forecasting technique used to make predictions about future values in a time series dataset. In this case, the app will use historical crime data from the Pittsburgh Police Department to generate predictions about future crime occurrences. These predictions will be updated regularly, taking into account recent crime trends and patterns.

Users of the app will be able to access information about crime predictions in their neighborhood and other areas in Pittsburgh. They will also receive safety tips and recommendations on how to avoid potential risks based on the predicted crime occurrences. The app will also feature an emergency button, which users can use to alert authorities in case of an emergency.

The Yinzer security app that uses time series models to predict crime in Pittsburgh has the potential to help people make more informed decisions about security. It aims to provide users with accurate crime predictions, safety tips, and recommendations on how to avoid potential risks. With its emergency button feature and the ability to learn from user feedback, the app could become an essential tool for individuals, businesses, and law enforcement agencies in the city.
